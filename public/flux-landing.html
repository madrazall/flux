What's inside
01
A schedule that moves with you
Build your day in blocks — drag to reorder when things shift. Time is a suggestion, structure is the point.
02
Tasks that carry forward
Undone tasks roll to tomorrow automatically when you archive the day. A quiet asterisk after a few days — a nudge, nothing more.
03
Tags that earn their place
Start with four defaults. Type any tag you need — use it enough and it becomes a real category with its own colour and tracking.
04
End of day debrief
Wins, hard stuff, brain dump. Three fields. Archive the day and tomorrow starts clean.
05
A calendar that finds you
Add an appointment months out — it shows up in your day when it matters. You don't have to remember to check.
06
Patterns, on your terms
A separate tab that only unlocks after a few days. Energy trends, recurring friction, where your time actually goes. There when you want it.
WHY
Most planners are built for people who don't need planners. Flux is built around the reality that some days the structure is what gets you through — and the data is what helps you build better days.
